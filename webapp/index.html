Cathode Degradation Diagnostics

Na-ion capacity fade from explicit SEI, P2-O2, Jahn-Teller, desolvation, rate-stress, and bounded residual terms.

Simulation Parameters
Temperature 45°C
Operating temperature. Higher values accelerate SEI growth via Arrhenius kinetics (k = A·exp(-Ea/RT)). Indian summer ambient baseline is 45°C.
C-Rate 1.0C
Charge/discharge rate relative to capacity. Higher C-rates induce sodium metal deposition and accelerated mechanical stress on cathode particles. Industrial BMS typically operates at 0.5–2C.
Cycles 500
Total charge-discharge cycles to simulate. The browser mirror advances explicit cycle increments with parabolic SEI growth, Butler-Volmer overpotential, and a bounded residual term.
Diagnostics Composition
Na_x(Mn,Fe,D)O2 controls SOC window, P2-O2 onset, JT strain, desolvation, and voltage fade.
P2→O2 Phase Transition Enables the prismatic-to-octahedral structural phase transition ODE term for Na-ion cathodes. This captures the irreversible capacity loss during high-voltage cycling above 4.0V where the P2 layered structure transforms to O2.
Jahn-Teller Distortion Couples Mn³⁺ Jahn-Teller distortion into the degradation rate. In Mn-rich cathodes, the eg electron in the d⁴ configuration causes tetragonal elongation that accelerates lattice strain and capacity fade.
SEI Growth Model Solid Electrolyte Interphase growth follows √t kinetics with Arrhenius temperature dependence. The SEI layer consumes mobile sodium inventory, causing irreversible capacity loss proportional to film thickness.
Residual Correction Adds a bounded residual term for model mismatch. It is a correction channel, not a substitute for the explicit SEI, phase-transition, and distortion terms.
Parameterizable Degradation ODE
dQ/dN = -Q[ kSEI·d(√N)/dN + kP2·g(SOC,T,JT) + kJT·JT(SOC) + kdes·ln(1+DNa) + kBV·η² + r ]
SEI uses parabolic kinetics d(sqrt(N))/dN = 1/(2sqrt(N)). g(SOC,T,JT) = sigmoid((SOC-SOC*)/0.045) * exp(T stress) * (1+0.35*JT) * dopant suppression; Al mainly suppresses P2-O2, Ti mainly suppresses JT/rate stress. eta = asinh(C-rate/(2*i0_proxy)), where i0_proxy depends on Fe fraction, desolvation barrier, and charge-transfer beta. r is bounded (|r| <= 0.15*total_loss).

BMS Pack Monitoring

Pack thermal graph with ohmic heat, neighbor conduction, cooling, EIS drift, and multi-window risk smoothing.

BMS means Battery Management System. Here it answers one operational question: which cell is becoming unsafe early enough to cool, isolate, or inspect?

Pack Configuration
Pack Size 8 cells
Number of cells in the simulated pack. Edges represent adjacent thermal paths; each cell integrates heat generation, conduction, and cooling.
Pack Layout Assumptions
4S2P shares current inside each parallel group; series string uses uniform current. Thermal edges follow the drawn module layout.
Simulation Duration 120s
Monitoring duration in seconds. Each step updates temperature, SEI drift, charge-transfer resistance, and risk history.
Ambient Temperature 45 C
External pack environment used in the cooling term Ta. Hot ambient reduces thermal headroom and raises EIS-driven risk.
Inject Cell Failure Injects an accelerating resistance-growth fault into one random cell so the thermal and EIS risk logic has a stress case.
EIS Feature Extraction Adds Randles-style Rct and RSEI drift into the risk score. These features move before temperature alone becomes conclusive.
Asymmetric Alert Loss Uses the false-negative / false-positive cost ratio below to lower the action threshold when late detection is more costly than a false alarm.
Parameterizable Pack Thermal/Risk Equation
Cth dTi/dt = I²Ri + Σ kij(Tj-Ti) - h(Ti-Ta); risk_i(t) = f(T, dT/dt, Rct, RSEI, neighbor risk_i(t-dt))
Same seed = same result. Change seed to explore different fault scenarios.

Cathode Materials Discovery

Na-ion cathode screening by capacity, voltage, fade, defect risk, phase stability, oxygen risk, and estimated material cost.

Composition Space
Na Content 1.00
Sodium stoichiometry in Na_x(Mn,Fe)O2. Values near 1.0 represent a sodiated layered oxide; sodium deficiency increases phase and moisture sensitivity.
Mn Fraction 0.50
Manganese fraction in the transition-metal layer. Mn can support capacity, but Mn3+ raises Jahn-Teller distortion and oxygen-risk penalties.
Fe Fraction 0.50
Iron fraction for the Fe3+/Fe4+ redox contribution. Fe raises voltage but shifts charge balance and migration penalties.
Transition-metal site sum is normalized before scoring.
Dopant: Al3+ Substitution Aluminium doping stabilizes the layered structure by suppressing the P2->O2 phase transition. Al3+ occupies octahedral sites without participating in redox, acting as structural pillars during deep desodiation.
Dopant: Ti4+ Substitution Titanium doping introduces a d0 cation that suppresses Jahn-Teller distortion by diluting the Mn3+ concentration. It also improves rate capability through lower Na+ migration barriers.
Parameterizable Screening Objective
score = wcap*Q0 + wstab*stability + wfade*(1-fade500) - wcost*cost/kWh - charge/defect penalties

Recycling Optimization

Shrinking-core leaching model with acid order, particle-size dependence, Arrhenius temperature scaling, recovery priors, and feedstock uncertainty.

Feedstock Parameters
Black Mass (kg) 100
Total black-mass feedstock from shredded cells. Feedstock variation is propagated as assay and stream-composition uncertainty.
Acid Concentration (M) 2.0
Sulfuric acid molarity for hydrometallurgical leaching. The shrinking-core ODE model computes dissolution rate as a function of acid concentration, temperature, and particle size distribution.
Leaching Temperature 80°C
Leaching reactor temperature. Higher temperatures accelerate dissolution kinetics exponentially (Arrhenius) but increase energy cost and acid evaporation losses. Optimal range: 60-90°C for sulfuric acid route.
Monte Carlo Blending Samples feedstock and assay variation to estimate a recovery interval without changing the underlying kinetics.
Bayesian Prior Blending When enabled, blends literature-derived Beta-distributed priors — Beta(α, β) — with the shrinking-core kinetic estimate for each element. This regularizes recovery predictions when experimental data is limited.
Parameterizable Leaching Kinetics (Surface-Reaction Control)
1 - (1-X)^(1/3) = k0·Cacid^n·exp[-Ea/R(1/T-1/Tref)]·(50/Rp)^m·t
Particles below roughly 25 um use a mixed surface/diffusion-control correction instead of pure surface-reaction control.
